# Includer

**Domain:** Relationship Building

## Definition
People exceptionally talented in the Includer theme accept others. They show awareness of those who feel left out and make an effort to include them.

## Description
"Stretch the circle wider." This philosophy guides how Includer-talented individuals orient their lives. They actively work to include people and foster group belonging, specifically avoiding exclusive groups. The desire to expand participation so others benefit from group support is central to their approach. They instinctively experience discomfort seeing anyone excluded and seek to draw them into warmth and connection.

Their acceptance stems from viewing all people as fundamentally equal and deserving inclusion, regardless of background. They avoid making judgments that might hurt others' feelings.

How people with Includer describe themselves:
- "I am aware of social exclusion and understand its repercussions."
- "I need room for everyone."
- "I love assimilation and integration."
- "I hate cliques."
- "I bring high tolerance and acceptance of diversity."

## Working With
Consider assigning them responsibility for representing unheard voices on the team or organization. This provides meaningful satisfaction while ensuring broader inclusion and connection across the workplace.
