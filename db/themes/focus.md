# Focus

**Domain:** Executing

## Definition
People exceptionally talented in the Focus theme can take a direction, follow through and make the corrections necessary to stay on track. They prioritize, then act.

## Description
Focus individuals ask themselves "Where am I headed?" daily. They require clear destinations and set yearly, monthly, and weekly goals that serve as compasses for determining priorities. Their talent forces efficiency through filtering — evaluating whether actions support their objectives. This creates impatience with delays and obstacles, making them valuable team members who redirect others toward main objectives.

How people with Focus describe themselves:
- "I am deeply driven and single-minded."
- "I need a goal to establish priorities."
- "I love to begin with the end in mind."
- "I hate going off on misdirected tangents."
- "I bring clarity through concentration and direction."

## Working With
Their most valuable contribution involves helping others establish and work toward goals. On group projects, they should help summarize decisions, clarify action timelines, and set reconvening dates.

## Needs
A clear goal or destination to work toward. Without direction, Focus talents can become frustrated or impatient. Provide well-defined initiatives and the freedom to reject misaligned projects.
