# Input

**Domain:** Strategic Thinking

## Definition
People exceptionally talented in the Input theme have a need to collect and archive. They may accumulate information, ideas, artifacts or even relationships.

## Description
Input individuals are inquisitive collectors. They gather "information -- words, facts, books, and quotations" or tangible objects. The world appeals to them because of "its infinite variety and complexity." They retain materials without knowing exact future uses, believing items "might prove valuable." Reading and travel serve collection purposes rather than theory refinement.

How people with Input describe themselves:
- "I am a collector of resources, information and objects that interest me."
- "I need space to store the resources I naturally acquire."
- "I love to provide relevant and tangible help."
- "I hate not having things that someone could use."
- "I bring tangible tools that can facilitate growth and performance."

## Working With
Coach them to recognize when sufficient information exists. Help set time limits on their research so they and their team can act on their findings.

## At Their Best
Input talents thrive when transforming collected knowledge into actionable insights benefiting others, positioning themselves as field authorities through their expertise.
