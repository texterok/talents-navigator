# Responsibility

**Domain:** Executing

## Definition
People exceptionally talented in the Responsibility theme take psychological ownership of what they say they will do. They are committed to stable values such as honesty and loyalty.

## Description
The theme forces individuals to take psychological ownership for commitments and feel emotionally bound to completion. Their reputation becomes tied to delivery. When unable to fulfill obligations, they seek restitution rather than accepting apologies. This conscientiousness and ethics create a reputation for dependability, making them first choices for responsibility assignments. However, their willingness to volunteer may lead to overcommitment.

How people with Responsibility describe themselves:
- "I am someone others trust to get things done."
- "I need freedom to take ownership."
- "I love the respect of others."
- "I hate disappointing others and being disappointed by others."
- "I bring dependability and loyalty."

## Working With
Individuals with strong Responsibility define themselves through commitment fulfillment. They become frustrated with colleagues lacking similar values and work best when avoiding teams with uncommitted members. Be mindful of their tendency to over-volunteer — help them protect their capacity.
