# Competition

**Domain:** Influencing

## Definition
People exceptionally talented in the Competition theme measure their progress against the performance of others. They strive to win first place and revel in contests.

## Description
The theme centers on comparative assessment. Competition-oriented individuals instinctively track peer performance as their reference point. Achievements only feel meaningful when they surpass others' results. These individuals require competitors and measurement systems to fuel their drive. They gravitate toward competitive environments and contests with clear winners, particularly when positioned advantageously. While respectful toward fellow competitors, they compete specifically to win rather than for enjoyment alone. Over time, they typically avoid competitions where victory appears unlikely.

How people with Competition describe themselves:
- "I am aware of my competitors."
- "I need peers for comparison and motivation."
- "I love a chance to go against the best."
- "I hate coming in second."
- "I bring an aspiration to be the best."

## Working With
Create daily success metrics for employees with high Competition talents. Help them identify what consistently produces wins within their specific roles.
